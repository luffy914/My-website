Tree and Plant Information

Photosynthesis:

Photosynthesis is the essential biological process by which green plants, algae, and certain bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy, creating glucose and oxygen from sunlight, water , and carbon dioxide. Occurring primarily in chloroplasts, this two-stage process (light-dependent reactions and the Calvin cycle) powers nearly all ecosystems on Earth.

Key Facts About Photosynthesis

Importants about Plants and trees
  • Light-dependent reactions capture sunlight to produce ATP and NADPH while splitting water molecules to release oxygen.
  • The Calvin cycle uses ATP and NADPH to convert \(CO_{2}\) into glucose through a series of enzyme-driven steps.
  • Photosynthesis is vital for life on Earth, providing oxygen for respiration and forming the base of food chains.
  • Human activities, such as deforestation and pollution, threaten photosynthetic organisms, impacting global oxygen levels and carbon cycles.
  • Understanding photosynthesis is crucial for addressing climate change and developing sustainable energy solutions.
